Our Projects.

Water, Sanitation Hygiene (WASH)

(a).Bio sand filter project . Since 2018, this has been a collaborative project between Daho foundation and Horizons Foundation (New York-USA). This projects involves provision, training on bio sand filters to community members in rural Kenya, where there is source of water like rivers, streams. The bio sands filters work by filtering the water from the sources making it safe for communities to use for both domestic and household.

(B).Every child .(hand washing) This a collaborative project between Daho foundation and SPATAP(Australia &United Kingdom). Since 2019, the project entails training school children on conservation of water , and also making possible for schools to practice hand washing, have clean safe water and sanitation facilities .SPATAP(Australia &United Kingdom) provides the spa tap water taps to Daho foundation , which are then supplied to schools for the school children to use for hand washing and water conservation, and also in partnership with other stakeholder’s refurbish water and sanitation facilities within the school.

( c)..Amua shops-this also a collaborative project by DAHO foundation that involves setting up of water kiosks and sanitation blocks in the community through a micro- enterprise model and partnership with community groups , who are trained and supported to run this facilities .

Education

Daho is determined to eradicate illiteracy through through various programs and projects in the target communities through easy access and quality education to all community members.under this DAHO , in Partnership and collaboration with various stakeholders have continued to serve target groups through its “Reach for the Stars” project..

(a).Reach for stars- this project by Daho foundation that involves education, entrepreneurship targeting street families in urban centers , whereas providing computer training and entrepreneurship skills training through its ones stop mobile school.

Empowerment

DAHO foundation and its collaborative partners intends to empower the community members through various projects and programs to eradicate various problems such as poverty, crime, diseases, social justice, inequalities.

(a)Justice and Rescue: The proposed project by DAHO foundation is aimed at promotion of women and youth empowerment through skills development and vocational trainings. This will be done through promotion of women and youth participation through capacity building and skills trainings to be independent among five selected villages in KENYA. The proposed project by Daho foundation is championing for the empowerment of women/youths by building their capacities and competencies. This will be achieved by conducting a six month period skills and entrepreneurship trainings which will equip them with the necessary skills and experience to manage their own businesses.

(b)Vijana Film project: The proposed project by Daho foundation is to be established as social entrepreneurship project with the sole aim of intervening on the country’s challenge of youth unemployment. The project mandate is therefore to facilitate creation of employment opportunities through enterprise development. The main identifiable challenge is insufficient funding for production of quality films; this affects the pre-production, production and the post production stages. The youth have shied away from film making due to the high costs of production coupled with the lack of finance sources for it. Other major challenges include; limited capacities to produce quality films and lack of distribution networks. Based on this premise, the aim of the partnership between the institutions is to address the challenges faced by youth in media productions and create an opportunity for the youth to invest and benefit from film making ventures.

One village , 100 computers: this a project by Daho Foundation “With ultimate goal to Reduce Unemployment and better marketing strategies using information technologies while refashioning urban informal and rural settlements. The 1 village 100 computers is a self-sustainable project providing free computer training and entrepreneurship for disadvantaged youths/community members in both urban informal and rural settlements in KENYA. At completion of the and mentorship, loans them computers and other technological devices to help them set up a computer based agri-bu resource centers such as cybercafé, respectively to the business plan designed during the training/mentorship. Within 6 months 30 youths in 3 groups with 10 members per group receive free Computer training in applications related agri-business services. Simultaneously, these youths receive an entrepreneurship mentorship - Each group is requested to identify a local mentor the Project assigns another mentor to each Group (a mentor: An experienced business person with 15+ years of diver business experience). These mentors help the group members to complete 9 modules course intended to help them c business plan for the group and to document this plan with an elevator pitch, a business plan summary, and an Invest Profile. At completion of this training & mentorship, each group (Leader) present their Business Plan Summary to Project and Leadership, mentors, and invited audience in a Public graduation conference. Each group then receive a loan package equipments worth not exceeding 3200 $, respectively to their business plan. On top, they receive project personnel w with them for the business development until the loan is fully repaid. For self-sustainability, the program requires loan repayment with interests of 12-15% if paid within 6 months and 12 months respectively.
